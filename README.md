# Early Appointment

**Clinic appointment booking & live queue-tracking web app**

Built in English & Hindi (see [pramarsh](https://github.com/yadavanujaditya/pramarsh) for the Hindi version).

---

## The Problem

During COVID (2020), my grandfather needed to see a local practitioner. When I called the clinic, they said: *"We don't take online bookings. You have to come in person."* Patients had no way to book ahead or know their position in the queue — they just showed up and waited.

## What I Built

A full-stack web app that lets patients:
- **Book appointments online** with local clinics
- **See their live queue position** — no more sitting in a crowded waiting room guessing
- **View estimated per-patient time** so they can arrive close to their slot

And lets clinics:
- Manage their daily appointment slots
- Update queue status in real time

## Why This Matters (to me)

I was a third-year MBBS student with **zero coding knowledge**. No HTML, no terminal, nothing. I taught myself full-stack web development from scratch in roughly **3 months** — entirely self-driven, no bootcamp, **pre-AI era** — specifically to build this.

This was my first line of code, and it became a fully working, deployed application.

## Current Status

**Ceased operations.** College reopened after COVID, and I couldn't find co-founders to carry it forward while resuming clinical training. I made the call to wind it down rather than half-run it.

The engineering foundation and the lessons carried directly into my current products — [DnbProPrep.com](https://dnbproprep.com) and [MoProPrep.in](https://moproprep.in).

## What I Learned

- Full-stack development end to end: frontend → API → database → deployment
- Building for real users with a real problem, not a tutorial project
- When to shut something down and move on
